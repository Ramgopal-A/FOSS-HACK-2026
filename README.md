ElderConnect 🏠
Seniors' Low-Cognitive-Load Social and Safety Gateway

📌 Summary
An open-source "digital companion" called ElderConnect was created especially for senior citizens who find contemporary smartphones intimidating. By eliminating "dark patterns," high-velocity "slop," and complicated navigation, it streamlines the digital experience and substitutes a big-button interface with high trust for necessary connectivity.

🚀 The Issue
Contemporary messaging apps and social media platforms are made to encourage "dopamine loops" and high levels of engagement, which frequently result in:

Seniors who feel "left behind" by complicated UI updates are experiencing digital isolation.

Scams & Misinformation: Since they don't have a "safe zone," they are susceptible to phishing and AI-generated fake news.

Cognitive Overload: Anxiety rather than connection is caused by an excessive number of notifications and hidden menus.

🛠️ Important Features
Dashboard simplification: Big icons for verified news, health alerts, and important contacts.

Anti-Scam Filter: Prevents users from seeing dubious "Urgent" messages or unreliable links by using local artificial intelligence.

By enabling a designated family member to remotely help with app settings or delete spam, shared-trust mode makes sure the elderly person is never "stuck."

Voice-First Navigation: Reduces the need for exact typing by optimizing for voice commands in regional languages.

Privacy-First: All private information and family communications remain local to the device (such as the user's phone ).

🏗️ Tech Stack Frontend: Tailwind CSS/React (optimized for large tap targets and high contrast)

Desktop/Mobile Framework: React Native or Electron

Node.js is the backend (local-first database).

AI Layer: NLP for message classification that is lightweight and offline-capable.

📅 Roadmap (FOSS Hack 2026)
Week 1: UI/UX Research: Establishing the boilerplate and determining the "Core Actions" for seniors.

Week 2: Developing the "Safe-Feed" aggregator, which incorporates verified news and family updates.

Week 3: Creating the protocol for "Shared-Trust" remote help.

Week 4: Demo recording and final accessibility testing (color blindness, motor-skill lag).
